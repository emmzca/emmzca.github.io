About

Thor Ragnarok follows the story of Thor fighting against the 'goddess of death', Hela, who wants to conquer all. She is one of the most powerful forces that Thor has encountered, as it is revealed that she is a very significant Asgardian as well, just like Thor. She destroys the Mjolnir very easily when first encountering Thor, showing how powerful she is. Hela also manages to damage Thor and Loki, sending them into space and ending up on the planet Sakaar. Here, Thor finds his old friend, Bruce Banner, but as the Hulk on the planet. He also learns that the person who captured him and brought him on the planet, Valkyrie, was once an Asgardian too. From here, Thor assembles a team named 'the revengers'including, his brother Loki, Valkyrie and the Hulk to escape Sakaar and defeat Hela. This movie has been critically acclaimed as being the most successful Thor movie out of the trilogy. However, there has also been opinions about how it was too much of a comedy and ruining the serious side that the previous Thor movies has established.

Significant Items

Eternal Flame

The eternal flame is a mystical, inextinguishable flame that contained the true power of the Fire Giant Surtur, and is capable of resurrecting deceased individuals. In the movie, it is used to resurect Surtur after Thor defeated him, unleasing Ragnarok on Asgard, living out the prophecy, to destroy Hela, but at the same time it also caused Asgard to fall. Hela also uses it to resurect her army from a long time ago, to fight with her against 'the revengers'. It was in the possesion of Odin, on Asgard, alone with other significant artifacts in the MCU, that were breifly mentioned in the movie. Such as, the tesseract in The Avengers and the infinity gauntlet that relates to infinity stones seen in various other movies.
