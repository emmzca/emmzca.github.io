About

In this movie, Jane Foster, who Thor met when he was banished to Earth in the first Thor movie, is under the influence of the aether because of the Dark Elves. After the Dark Elves got the aether in possesion, they wanted to use its power to bring back darkness to the world. After Jane was 'attacked' by the aether, because they gain life force from a host, Thor manages to save her and works with her and his brother Loki to come up with a plan to defeat the dark elves. The aether plays a very important role in this movies as it is also one of the infinity stones. However, this one is unique as it is in the form of a liquid rather than a stone.

Significant Items

Aether

The Aether is an Infinity Stone, also known as the reality stone, the remnant of a singularity that predates the universe. A force of ancient and catastrophic power, the Aether has the potential to transform the entire universe. This "stone" is unique since it is in the form of a dark red, viscous liquid, rather than a literal stone, like the other five. However, evidence from the Collector's Museum, suggests the Aether may not have always been this way and may have originally been a stone similar to the others. It is currently located in the Collector's Museum. The Aether represents the Infinity Stone of Reality.
